What is the acid test?
The acid test ratio is a ratio that compares the firm's cash and accounts receivable to it's current liabilities. The acid test ratio indicates whether the company has enough short term assets to cover it's short term liabilities. How to calculate acid test?
To calculate acid test you should use the following formula:
acid test = (cash + marketable securities + accounts receivable) / current liabilities
Why use acid test?
The acid test ratio is used to determine the short term liquidity of a company because it indicates whter the company has enough short term assets to cover it's short term liabilities
